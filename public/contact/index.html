What to include in an enquiry

Drop the points below in an email or repeat them on the phone. The more I know up front, the faster I can quote and the more accurate the number will be.

  • The property. Address or, if it’s pre-purchase and discreet, postcode and approximate age.
  • The service you think you need. Building survey, EPC, retrofit assessment, ventilation test — or “not sure”. I can help with the last one.
  • What you’re trying to do. Buying, selling, letting, refinancing, renovating, building from scratch, making it warmer, sorting damp.
  • The timeline. Exchange date, marketing date, mortgage offer expiry, planning condition deadline. If you’re flexible, say so.
  • Anything I should know. Listed status. Recent works. Known damp or movement. Tenant in situ. Difficult access. The bit the agent didn’t mention.

What you’ll get back

  • A short reply confirming I can help.
  • A fixed fee for the work.
  • A site-visit date that works around yours.
  • A clear scope, in plain English, of what you’ll be paying for.

If anything in the brief means I’m not the right person for the job, I’ll say so — and where possible, point you at someone who is.
